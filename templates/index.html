Question

Are the DGP of different country distributed even or biased?
If not even, in which region, the countries in it has more evenly distributed GDP?

Answer

I accumulate the GDP in 2017 of every country (region) across the world. The data was attributed to Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(nominal)). To answer our first question, we should look at a little bit at the data we collected. To better illustrate the skew in the GDP distribution, I drew a histogram from the data to show the distribution (see figure 1).

The result shows that there is a high skew in GDP distribution. The U.S. and China is far from other countries in terms of their economy.

This question is quite simple to answer. However, given we know that the knowledge that GDP is not distributed evenly, one may ask which region has a more evenly distributed GDP?

To understand relation between GDP and geometry, I plotted a heatmap of country distribution as follows. More countries in a region, warmer the color will be.

I also plotted a heatmap of heatmap of country distribution, but weighed by the GDP. This heatmap basically reflects how total GDP of a region has. Larger the GDP is in a region, warmer the color will be.

For one region we want to investigate, we see the heatmap of it in both figures. If the trends of color are in the same direction, we can say that GDP is relatively evenly distributed. Taking western Europe for example. Two figures looks basically the same.

Heatmap (weighed by GDP)
